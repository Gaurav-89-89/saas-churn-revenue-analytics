# Insights & Recommendations

## 1. Onboarding window is the highest-risk period
Churned customers leave in an average of 7.5 months, compared to 15.7 months for customers who stay active. The first several months of the customer lifecycle are where retention is won or lost.

**Recommendation:** Build a structured 90-day onboarding program — guided setup, milestone check-ins, and proactive customer success touchpoints — targeting new signups before they reach the high-risk window.

## 2. Low product usage is the strongest churn driver
Customers with under 30% feature adoption churn at 18.8%, compared to just 1.73% for customers with over 70% adoption — nearly an 11x difference. This is the single strongest signal in the dataset.

**Recommendation:** Set up automated usage-tracking alerts that trigger in-app prompts, tutorial nudges, or a customer success email when a customer's adoption drops below a threshold, rather than waiting until they've already disengaged.

## 3. Basic-tier, monthly-billed customers are the highest-risk segment
Basic plan churn (11.66%) is nearly double Enterprise churn (6.83%), and Monthly-billed customers churn almost twice as often as Annual-billed customers.

**Recommendation:** Offer a limited-time incentive (discount or bonus feature access) for Monthly customers to switch to Annual billing — this reduces churn risk and improves revenue predictability simultaneously.

## 4. A concrete, immediately actionable at-risk list exists today
647 currently active customers show both low feature adoption and high recent support ticket volume — representing $147,193 in monthly recurring revenue at risk.

**Recommendation:** Hand this segment directly to the Customer Success team as a prioritized outreach list. This is the fastest way to convert this analysis into real revenue protection.

## 5. Support tickets are a leading indicator, not just an operational metric
Churn rate rises consistently as the number of support tickets in the last 90 days increases.

**Recommendation:** Investigate root causes behind tickets from at-risk accounts — high ticket volume paired with low usage may indicate a product friction or onboarding gap, not just a support quality issue.

## Bottom Line
This analysis identified $457,926 in monthly recurring revenue lost to churn, with the strongest predictive signal being low product usage — customers with under 30% feature adoption churn at nearly 11 times the rate of highly engaged customers. A targeted onboarding program combined with proactive outreach to the 647 identified at-risk accounts (worth $147K/month) represents the most immediate opportunity to reduce revenue churn.
